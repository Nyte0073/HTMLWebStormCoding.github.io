Question: For each triangle, calculate the value of 'w' to the nearest tenth of a metre.
Trig Ratios
  • Sin of theta
  • Cos of theta
  • Tan of theta
Step 1: Declare Variables
  • tan 48 degrees = 5.5 / L, L = 5.5 / tan 48 degrees
  • tan 40 degrees = 5.5 / i, i = 5.5 / tan 40 degrees
  • w = i - l
L - Length of the 2nd triangle (48 degrees) that does NOT include the length of 'w'.

i - Length of the base triangle (40 degrees) that does include the length of 'w'.

w - i (total length) subtract L (known length, without the length of 'w' to give the value of 'i').
Step 2: Calculations
L = 5.5 / 1.1106, L = 4.95 metres
i = 5.5 / 0.8390, i = 6.55 metres
w = i - L = 6.55 - 4.95 = 1.6 metres
